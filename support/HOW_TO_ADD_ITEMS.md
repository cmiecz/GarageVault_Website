## Quick Start: Adding Your First Item

### Basic Steps

1. Open **Garage Vault**
2. Tap the **"+"** button (usually at bottom right or top)
3. Choose **"Add Item Manually"** or **"Scan Item with AI"**
4. Fill in the item details
5. Tap **"Save"**

That's it! Your item is now in your inventory.

## Two Ways to Add Items

### Method 1: Manual Entry (Traditional)

Best for when you:
- Already know all the item details
- Want complete control over what's entered
- Don't have the item's packaging or labels

**Steps:**
1. Tap **"+"** ‚Üí **"Add Item Manually"**
2. Fill in all fields (see sections below)
3. Tap **"Save"**

### Method 2: AI-Powered Scanning (Smart & Fast)

Best for when you:
- Have the item or its packaging
- Want to save time typing
- Need help identifying model/serial numbers

**Steps:**
1. Tap **"+"** ‚Üí **"Scan Item with AI"**
2. Point camera at the item, label, or packaging
3. Take a clear photo
4. AI automatically extracts:
   - Model numbers
   - Serial numbers
   - Brand names
   - Product details
5. Review and edit the auto-filled information
6. Tap **"Save"**

**Pro Tip:** For tools and equipment, you can scan multiple photos (front, back, label) for better detail extraction!

## Core Fields (All Items)

These fields are available for every item, regardless of category:

### Basic Information

**Item Name** *(Required)*
- What you call the item
- Examples: "Milwaukee M18 Impact Driver", "Deck Screws 3-inch", "Red Paint - Living Room"
- Tip: Be descriptive so you can find it easily later

**Category** *(Required)*
- Select from dropdown:
  - Tools
  - Fasteners
  - Paint
  - Automotive Fluids
  - Equipment
  - Custom categories you've created
- Tip: Choose carefully - this determines which special fields appear!

**Brand**
- Manufacturer name
- Examples: Milwaukee, DeWalt, Makita, Rust-Oleum, Mobil 1
- Tip: AI scanning often auto-fills this

**Model Number**
- Product model/SKU
- Examples: "2853-20", "DWE7491RS"
- Why it matters: Needed for warranty claims, finding manuals, ordering parts

**Description**
- Add any additional details
- Examples: "Birthday gift from dad", "Use this for outdoor projects only", "Needs blade sharpening"

### Quantity & Units

**Quantity** *(Required)*
- How many you have
- Examples: 1, 5, 250
- Updates automatically when you log usage or add more

**Unit**
- What you're counting
- Options: pieces, boxes, gallons, quarts, cans, liters, pounds, ounces, sets, pairs
- Examples: 
  - Screws: 100 pieces
  - Paint: 2 gallons
  - Motor oil: 4 quarts

**Low Stock Threshold**
- Alert you when quantity drops below this number
- Example: Set threshold to 10, get notified when you have fewer than 10 left
- Tip: Great for consumables like screws, paint, fluids

### Images

**Photos** *(Highly Recommended)*
- Free tier: 1 photo per item
- Premium: Unlimited photos
- Tips:
  - Take photos in good lighting
  - Capture front, back, and any labels
  - Photo the manual or important stickers
  - Document condition for loans
- Tap **camera icon** to add photos

**Image Tags & Captions**
- Tag photos: Front, Back, Manual, Receipt, Damage, Label
- Add captions: "Serial number location", "Damage from drop"

### Storage Location

**Where is it stored?**
- Select from your storage locations
- Examples: "Red Toolbox", "Garage Shelf 2", "Packout Drawer 3"
- Leave blank if not using storage tracking

**Storage Position**
- Exact spot within the storage location
- Examples: "Top drawer", "Slot 3", "Back left corner"
- Tip: Makes finding items super fast!

### Loan Status

**Current Status**
- Available (ready to use/loan)
- Loaned (someone borrowed it)
- Borrowed (you borrowed from someone else)

**If Loaned/Borrowed:**
- Who has it
- Expected return date
- Contact information
- Condition when loaned
- Tip: Great for tracking tools borrowed by neighbors!

## üîß Tools Category

### When to Use
- Power tools (drills, saws, sanders)
- Hand tools (wrenches, screwdrivers, hammers)
- Pneumatic tools
- Measuring tools
- Tool accessories

### Special Fields

**Tool Type**
- Drill, Impact Driver, Circular Saw, Miter Saw, Sander, Grinder, Wrench Set, Socket Set, Hammer, etc.
- Helps organize and filter your tools

**Battery Platform** *(For cordless tools)*
- M12, M18 (Milwaukee)
- 20V MAX, FlexVolt (DeWalt)
- 18V LXT (Makita)
- And many more
- Why it matters: Know which batteries work with which tools!

**Voltage**
- 12V, 18V, 20V, 40V, 60V, 120V, 240V
- Important for cordless tools and power requirements

**Serial Number** *(Important!)*
- Unique identifier stamped on the tool
- Why it matters:
  - Warranty claims require it
  - Police recovery if stolen
  - Prove ownership
- Tip: Take a photo of the serial number plate!

**Size/Specifications**
- Examples: "1/2 inch chuck", "7-1/4 inch blade", "6-inch"
- Any relevant measurements or specs

**Condition**
- New, Like New, Good, Fair, Poor
- Update as tool wears over time

## üî© Fasteners Category

### When to Use
- Screws (wood, metal, drywall)
- Nails
- Bolts
- Nuts
- Washers
- Anchors
- Rivets

### Special Fields

**Fastener Type**
- Screw, Nail, Bolt, Nut, Washer, Anchor, Rivet, Staple
- Primary fastener classification

**Size**
- Examples: "#8 x 2-1/2 inch", "1/4-20", "16d", "M8"
- Include thread size and length

**Material**
- Steel, Stainless Steel, Brass, Aluminum, Galvanized, Zinc-Plated
- Important for outdoor vs indoor use

**Thread Type** *(For screws/bolts)*
- Coarse, Fine, Machine, Wood, Self-Tapping
- Determines what materials you can use it with

**Drive Type**
- Phillips, Torx, Hex, Square (Robertson), Flathead, Pozidriv
- What bit you need to drive it

**Head Type**
- Flat, Pan, Round, Oval, Hex, Button, Truss
- Flush mount vs raised head

**Finish**
- Galvanized, Zinc-Plated, Black Oxide, Stainless, Brass, Plain
- Affects rust resistance

**Package Count**
- How many pieces came in the original package
- Example: "Box of 100", "Bag of 250"
- Helps calculate remaining quantity

## üöó Automotive Fluids Category

### When to Use
- Motor oil
- Coolant/Antifreeze
- Brake fluid
- Transmission fluid
- Power steering fluid
- Windshield washer fluid
- Gear oil
- Hydraulic fluid

### Special Fields

**Fluid Type**
- Motor Oil, Coolant, Brake Fluid, Transmission Fluid, Power Steering, Windshield Washer, Gear Oil, Hydraulic Fluid, Grease

**Viscosity Grade**
- Examples: 5W-30, 10W-40, 0W-20, 75W-90, DOT 3, DOT 4
- Critical for proper engine/system performance
- Must match vehicle specifications!

**Application**
- What it's used for
- Examples: "Engine", "Transmission", "Brakes", "Differential", "Transfer Case"

**SAE Rating** *(For oils)*
- Society of Automotive Engineers rating
- Examples: SAE 5W-30, SAE 15W-40

**Specification** *(For oils)*
- API, ILSAC, ACEA ratings
- Examples: "API SN", "ILSAC GF-6", "ACEA A3/B4"
- Why it matters: Vehicle manufacturers require specific specs

**Synthetic/Conventional**
- Full Synthetic, Synthetic Blend, Conventional
- Affects change intervals and performance

**Expiration Date** *(Important!)*
- Fluids can degrade over time
- Typically 3-5 years for unopened oil
- 6 months - 2 years for coolant once opened
- Dashboard alerts you before expiration

**Compatible Vehicles**
- List vehicles this fluid works with
- Examples: "2018 F-150 5.0L", "Lawn Tractor", "All vehicles"

## Tips for Effective Item Entry

### Photography Best Practices

‚úÖ **DO:**
- Take photos in bright, natural lighting
- Capture serial number plates clearly
- Photo the original packaging/label
- Include all sides of power tools
- Photograph manuals or instruction stickers
- Show any damage or wear

‚ùå **DON'T:**
- Take photos in dark or shadowy areas
- Use flash (can cause glare on labels)
- Skip photographing serial numbers
- Forget to photo receipts before throwing away

### Naming Conventions

**Good Names:**
- "Milwaukee M18 Hammer Drill 2804-20"
- "Deck Screws 3-inch Torx GRK R4"
- "Sherwin Williams Naval SW 6244 - Kitchen"
- "Mobil 1 5W-30 Full Synthetic"

**Bad Names:**
- "Drill" (too vague)
- "Screws" (which size?)
- "Blue Paint" (what room? what brand?)
- "Oil" (for what?)

**Tips:**
- Include brand and model
- Add size or key spec
- For paint: include room used
- For fluids: include viscosity
- Be specific enough to distinguish from similar items

### Quantity Tracking Strategy

**Set Low Stock Thresholds For:**
- Consumables (screws, nails, paint)
- Fluids you use regularly
- Replacement parts (blades, filters)
- Items you never want to run out of

**Don't Bother Tracking Quantity For:**
- One-off tools (you have 1, you'll always have 1)
- Items you rarely use
- Non-consumables

### Organization Tips

üí° **Enter Items Immediately**
- Add items as soon as you buy them
- Use AI scanning while still in parking lot
- Scan receipt before putting it away

üí° **Batch Entry**
- Adding many items? Set aside 30 minutes
- Gather everything, take photos, enter all at once
- Use AI scanning to speed it up

üí° **Start with High-Value Items**
- Tools and equipment first (warranty, theft recovery)
- Consumables can wait
- Focus on items worth $50+

üí° **Use Consistent Storage Names**
- Name storage locations before adding items
- Use QR codes on physical storage
- Always put items in same place

## AI Scanning Tips

### Best Results from AI Scanning

**For Tools/Equipment:**
- Scan the main label with model number
- Take additional photo of serial number plate
- Photo of specification sticker
- AI extracts: brand, model, serial, specs

**For Fasteners:**
- Scan the package label
- Get close enough to read text clearly
- AI extracts: size, type, quantity, brand

**For Paint:**
- Scan the lid or label
- Include color code and name
- Photo of batch/formula sticker inside lid
- AI extracts: brand, color name, color code, type

**For Automotive Fluids:**
- Scan the front label
- Get close to see specifications
- AI extracts: brand, viscosity, type, specs

**For Equipment:**
- Scan multiple angles (front, side, engine)
- Photo all labels and plates
- Include manual if available
- AI extracts: brand, model, serial, engine size

### When AI Makes Mistakes

- Review all auto-filled information
- Correct any errors before saving
- AI is smart but not perfect!
- Double-check serial numbers especially
